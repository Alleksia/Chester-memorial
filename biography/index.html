Biography

Early Life

Chester Charles Bennington was born on March 20, 1976 in Phoenix, Arizona. His mother was a nurse, and his father was a police detective who specialized in child sexual abuse cases. Chester attended Greenway High School.

From the moment he learned to talk, Chester told everyone he would grow up to be a singer. His older brother played Foreigner records constantly, and by age two, Chester was running around the house singing their songs. His mother recalls how he would perform all the parts from the 1980 musical "Popeye" for anyone who visited.

Chester's parents divorced when he was 11 years old. He was bullied at school and struggled with feelings of isolation. Music became his escape.

First Steps in Music

As Chester grew older, he developed his distinctive singing style.

"I did it all the wrong way. I basically went at it till it didn't hurt anymore. It's something I've been blessed with."

His favorite band was Ministry, and he tried to mimic Al Jourgensen's compressed vocals without effects. He also listened to Depeche Mode, The Cure, The Misfits, and The Smiths.

In high school, Chester got into musical theater and traveled the country with a stage production of "You Are The Child."

Grey Daze

In 1993, Chester joined the Phoenix-based band Grey Daze as lead vocalist. The band recorded two albums: "Wake Me" (1994) and "...No Sun Today" (1997).

Grey Daze built a strong local following in Arizona, but Chester grew frustrated with the band's lack of progress. He left Grey Daze in 1998, searching for greater opportunities.

Years later, after Chester's passing, the surviving members of Grey Daze completed and released unfinished recordings as a tribute album called "Amends" (2020).

The Birth of Linkin Park

In 1999, Chester received a call from Jeff Blue, a music executive who had seen his work with Grey Daze. Blue told him about a band called Xero that needed a new vocalist. Chester flew to Los Angeles and auditioned on his birthday.

He got the job immediately. The band changed their name from Xero to Hybrid Theory, and finally to Linkin Park — a nod to Lincoln Park in Santa Monica.

The lineup was complete: Chester Bennington (vocals), Mike Shinoda (vocals/rap/guitar), Brad Delson (guitar), Dave "Phoenix" Farrell (bass), Joe Hahn (turntables), and Rob Bourdon (drums).

Hybrid Theory · 2000

On October 24, 2000, Linkin Park released their debut album "Hybrid Theory". The album was a massive success, selling over 4.8 million copies in its first year and becoming the best-selling album of 2001 in the United States.

Singles like "One Step Closer", "Crawling", "Papercut", and "In the End" dominated radio and MTV. Chester's raw screams and vulnerable melodies became the voice of a generation struggling with alienation and pain.

"Crawling" won a Grammy Award for Best Hard Rock Performance in 2002.

Meteora · 2003

The follow-up album "Meteora" was released on March 25, 2003. It debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 and sold over 810,000 copies in its first week.

Hit singles included "Somewhere I Belong", "Faint", "Numb", "Breaking the Habit", and "From the Inside". "Numb" became one of the band's most iconic songs, with over 2 billion views on YouTube.

During the Meteora era, Chester struggled with health issues, including severe abdominal pain that required surgery while filming the "Numb" music video in Prague.

Minutes to Midnight · 2007

After a long break, Linkin Park returned with "Minutes to Midnight" on May 14, 2007. The album showed a more mature, politically conscious sound.

Lead single "What I've Done" became an anthem for environmental awareness. Other hits included "Bleed It Out", "Shadow of the Day", and "Leave Out All the Rest".

In October 2007, Chester broke his wrist during a show in Melbourne but continued to perform the entire set before going to the hospital.

Dead By Sunrise · 2009

Chester formed his side project Dead By Sunrise in 2005 with members of Julien-K. The band's debut album "Out of Ashes" was released on October 13, 2009.

The album was deeply personal, written during Chester's divorce and struggles with alcoholism. Singles included "Crawl Back In" and "Let Down".

A Thousand Suns · 2010

"A Thousand Suns" was released on September 8, 2010. It was a concept album about nuclear warfare and human conflict.

Singles included "The Catalyst", "Waiting for the End", "Burning in the Skies", and "Iridescent", which was featured in "Transformers: Dark of the Moon."

Living Things · 2012

"Living Things" was released on June 20, 2012. The album blended the electronic elements of "A Thousand Suns" with the heavier rock sound of their earlier work.

Singles included "Burn It Down", "Lost in the Echo", and "Castle of Glass".

Stone Temple Pilots · 2013–2015

In May 2013, Chester joined Stone Temple Pilots as their new lead vocalist, replacing the late Scott Weiland. He recorded the EP "High Rise" with the band, which included the single "Out of Time".

Chester left Stone Temple Pilots amicably in November 2015 to focus on Linkin Park.

The Hunting Party · 2014

"The Hunting Party" was released on June 13, 2014. It marked a return to the band's heavier rock roots, featuring guest appearances from rock legends like Tom Morello, Page Hamilton, and Daron Malakian.

Singles included "Guilty All the Same", "Until It's Gone", and "Final Masquerade".

One More Light · 2017

Linkin Park's seventh album "One More Light" was released on May 19, 2017. It featured a more pop-oriented sound and deeply personal lyrics.

The title track "One More Light" was written after the death of a friend at the band's record label. Chester struggled to perform it without breaking down.

Just two months after the album's release, Chester was gone.

Friendship with Chris Cornell

Chester was incredibly close with Chris Cornell of Soundgarden and Audioslave. They became friends during a tour in the mid-2000s. Chester was the godfather of Chris's son Christopher.

When Chris died by suicide on May 18, 2017, Chester was devastated. He wrote an open letter saying he couldn't imagine a world without Chris in it. He performed "Hallelujah" at Chris's funeral.

Family & Personal Life

Chester had six children. His first son, Jaime, was born in 1996. He married his first wife, Samantha Olit, in 1996, and they had a son named Draven in 2002. The couple divorced in 2005.

In 2005, Chester married Talinda Bentley, a former Playboy model. They had three children together: Tyler (2006) and twins Lily and Lila (2011). He also adopted his ex-partner's son, Isaiah.

Chester was a tattoo enthusiast with dozens of tattoos, many representing his struggles and triumphs. He was an avid fan of the Phoenix Suns, Arizona Cardinals, and Arizona Diamondbacks.

The Battle Within

Throughout his life, Chester struggled with depression, anxiety, and substance abuse. He was sexually abused as a child by an older friend, a trauma he carried for years before revealing it publicly.

He battled alcoholism multiple times, undergoing interventions and treatment. Despite his inner demons, he used his platform to help others, becoming a voice for those who felt voiceless.

Legacy

On July 20, 2017 — what would have been Chris Cornell's 53rd birthday — Chester Charles Bennington died by suicide at his home in Palos Verdes Estates, California. He was 41 years old.

"Who cares if one more light goes out? Well I do."

Chester's voice touched millions. His honesty about mental health inspired countless fans to seek help. Linkin Park's music continues to resonate with new generations. In 2024, the band announced their return with new vocalist Emily Armstrong, ensuring Chester's legacy lives on.

Chester Bennington · 1976–2017 · Forever in our hearts
